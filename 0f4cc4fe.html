  • Operator Fixity

    • Haskell# has a notion of operator fixity, which determines how tightly an infix operator binds to its arguments. Fixity ranges from 0 to 9 (inclusive). Low fixity means low binding priority (e.g. ($) has fixity 0 so all other operators are applied before it), and high fixity means high binding priority (e.g. (.) has fixity 9 so composition is applied before anything else).
