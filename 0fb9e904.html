Poisson Distribution

$$ P_\lambda(Z=k) = \frac{\lambda^ke^{-\lambda}}{k!}, k \in \mathbb{N} $$

The parameter \(\lambda\), called the “intensity” of the Poisson distribution, can take any positive number. Higher \(\lambda\) gives more weight to higher \(k\).

Properties

  • The expected value is equal to the intensity: \(E[Z|\lambda]=\lambda\)
