Binomial Distribution

$$ P_{N,p}(Z=k) = {N \choose k} p^k (1 - p)^{N-k} $$

\(Bin(N, 0.5)\) is like flipping a coin \(N\) times; \(N\) is the number of “trials” behind the distribution, and \(p\) is the probability of “the event” happening (simple yes or no) in a particular trial.

Via the 3blue1brown video: the binomial distribution’s PMF can answer the question, “how likely is it that I observe a particular success rate (\(k/N\)) given the true (latent) success rate (\(p\))?” For example, “how likely was it that 48 of this movie’s 50 reviews were positive, given we (somehow) know the true ‘like-rate’ was 0.95?”

from scipy import stats
stats.binom.pmf(k=48, n=50, p=0.95)  # 0.26110137039881826

To break it down, \(N \choose k\) is the number of ways to arrange \(k\) things into \(N\) slots, and \(p^k (1 - p)^k\) is the probability of any one of those arrangements.

Properties

  • \(E[Z|N,p] = Np\)
